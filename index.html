Boyz on foot vs. in a Transport

Having always been a fan of the Ork horde in movies and books and video games, its no wonder that I play Warhammer 40k the same way. The tactics for this to be effective have changed over the years as the rules have changed. As it stands now, transports are effective at keeping your boyz alive and delivering them to the enemies gates where they can do the most damage. Orks have one of the most durable transports in the game right now in the Battlewagon. At 16 wounds and a 20 boy capacity, it can also be kitted to deal out some serious firepower. Trukks are the other option and while cheap, they are not nearly as durable. They have a nice ability in ramshackle but it's not something that should be relied on. The major pitfall of these transports is their carrying ability. The Battlewagon carries 20 and the trukk carries 12. To be effective, Orks need maxed out squads right now which is 30. This makes their leadership basically a non-issue and they can hit like a train in HtH. Orks excel in foot-slogging horde mode right now so if you take transports, it will be to drive around units that can shoot on the move or to shotgun Nobz in your enemies face.

Trukks vs. Battlewagons

Trukks are better than ever. They are quick and open-topped so their ocupants can shoot while embarked. With 10 wounds and a 4+ save they are not the most durable transport. Ramshackle helps them survive, turning damage higher than 1 into a 1 on a roll of 6+ but its not something your boyz can rely on. Right now they drive my Tankbustas around the field because they are a cheap way to put a shield around a valuable unit. Battlewagons are monsters. With the capability to take 4 rokkits or big shootas AND a Killkannon, they can pack a punch. The deffrolla is also back and lets the Battlewagon engage the enemy effectively in HtH. Holding up to 20 models means this is the perfect delivery for my Nob squad to ride in. You have the option of taking them open-topped or with a 'Ard Case. The 'Ard Case is free and brings your toughness up to 8 so its a no brainer to take it. The Nobz won't be super effective with shooting out of it anyway. Best to get up in the enemies' faces and krump them gud!

Tankbustas!!

Tankbustas are one of the best ork units right now. They are normal boyz but they all have rokkit launchas. At Str 8, AP -2, and D 3 there is not much the tankbustas have trouble bringing down. Their special ability, however, is where they really shine. They can reroll failed to hit rolls when shooting at a vehicle. With a BS 5+ the reroll ensures they get enough hits in to bring down their target! Throw them in a Trukk or Battlewagon to have a super mobile armor deleting unit in your army!

Grots?

Grots are the wimpiest beings in the ork world and it's no different on the tabletop. What they are best for is sitting on objectives in your back lines as well as potentially soaking up overwatch on a charge. Operation Meat Shield is a real thing in grot life. Being extremely cheap is their main advantage. I usually run one minimum squad to get objectives but I have see some successes from others using them maxed out and on the front lines just being a nusance to the enemy.

Ghazghkull Mag Uruk Thraka

The Ork, The Myth, The Legend. This guy is a destroyer of worlds. Helping keep his already beastly stat line alive and bashing heads on the field he sports a 2+ save and 4+ invulnerable save. Throw in a Painboy and Tenacious survivor and he will tank some serious damage before falling. If that weren't enough, he boosts the boyz around him as well. The boyz within 6" will have +1 to their ATK characteristic when they charge and allows the boys to charge EVEN though they already advanced! Ghazghkull wants the boyz to cover ground quickly to get stuck in and his ability is perfect for that.

Funny Ork Conquests

Grizgutz

This Ork Warlord led his WAAAGH!!! into the Morloq system. Using warp travel, Grizgutz and his boyz teleported and when they came out of the warp Grizgutz realized he had traveled back in time to shortly before he was about to leave on his conquest. Being a kleptomaniac, Grizgutz decended on his own planet and killed himself to claim his own gun as a backup gun. The resulting confusion, however, sent his boys into a fury and the WAAAGH!!! was sent into disarray.

Tuska, Daemon-Killa

Tuska led his WAAAGH!!! into the Eye of Terror and made his name by defeating Daemon hordes on many Daemon worlds. His WAAAGH!!! came to an end when he landed on a flesh planet belonging to a Daemon Prince of Khorne known as the Blood Prince. After an epic battle, Tuska was finally slain by the wrathful Blood Prince. However, his story does not end there. The chaos God, Khorne, loved the battle so much he resurrected the Ork horde the next dawn to fight again. Now Tuska and his most battle hardened Nobz fight daily in the Brass Citadel against Khorne's elite Bloodletter generals.
